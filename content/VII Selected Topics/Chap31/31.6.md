## 31.6-3

> Assuming that you know $\phi(n)$, explain how to compute $a^{-1}~\text{mod}~n$ for any $a \in \mathbb{Z}\_n^*$ using the procedure MODULAR-EXPONENTIATION.

{equation}<div>
$$\begin{array}{rlll}
a^{\phi(n)} &\equiv& 1 &(\text{mod}~n) \\
a\cdot a^{\phi(n)-1} &\equiv& 1 &(\text{mod}~n) \\

\end{array}
